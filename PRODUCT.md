# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Tasteprint is for builders using AI coding agents to ship frontend work: founders, developers, designers, and product teams who know the taste they want but need it translated into agent-readable design context. They use it at the start of a project or before a serious redesign, when a messy product dump needs to become practical design direction.

## Product Purpose

Tasteprint is a local onboarding tool that interviews the user, learns their product context and references, then generates project-specific `DESIGN.md` and `SKILL.md` files for Claude Code, Gemini CLI, Codex CLI, Cursor, and similar agents. Success means the user leaves with a useful design system their coding agent can follow without turning the app into a generic template.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, and editorial. Tasteprint should feel like a serious design director turning rough taste into a polished artifact: premium and technical, but not cold; creative and opinionated, but not cute.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS gradients, loud rainbow AI branding, playful mascot onboarding, crypto/Web3 language, dense admin dashboards, Apple/Linear/Stripe/Runway clones, excessive cards, excessive copy, bouncy motion, and startup-template hero sections.

## Design Principles

- One screen, one decision.
- Show the product as a design tool, not an AI wrapper.
- Treat references as attributes to interpret, never brands to copy.
- Keep the primary accent scarce and meaningful.
- Make generated design context feel like a crafted artifact.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, visible keyboard focus, semantic selected states, and touch targets of at least 40px. Do not rely on color alone for selection. Respect reduced motion and keep motion purposeful, especially on repeated product interactions.
